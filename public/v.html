Ingredients That Require Slaughter of Cattle, Buffalo, or Calves

Products containing any of the ingredients below directly involve the killing of cows, buffalo, or calves. Use this table as a reference when shopping. (Dairy-only ingredients like whey, casein, or butter are not included). If an ingredient is ambiguous or could be from plants as well, verify with the manufacturer.

Ingredient Description/Usage Source Common Foods/Products
Beef Meat of adult cattle Slaughtered cows Burgers, steaks, soups, ready meals
Veal Meat of young calves Slaughtered calves Cutlets, scallopini, stews
Buffalo Meat Meat of buffalo Slaughtered buffalo Burgers, steaks, sausages
Oxtail Tail of cattle Slaughtered cows Oxtail soup, stews
Tripe Stomach lining Slaughtered cows Pho, menudo, stews
Sweetbreads Thymus/pancreas glands Slaughtered calves/cows French/Italian cuisine, appetizers
Tongue Tongue of cattle/veal Slaughtered cows, calves Deli meats, tacos, sandwiches
Liver Liver of cattle/veal Slaughtered cows, calves Pâté, liverwurst, supplements
Heart Heart muscle Slaughtered cows, calves Stews, specialty dishes
Kidneys Organs Slaughtered cows, calves Steak and kidney pie, stews
Blood/Blood Plasma Blood from slaughter Slaughtered cows, calves Sausages, black pudding, some processed meats
Bone Marrow Marrow inside bones Slaughtered cows, calves Roasted marrow, broth, pho, soups
Gelatin Collagen from bones/skin Slaughtered cows (sometimes pigs) Gummies, marshmallows, jellies, yogurt
Tallow Rendered beef fat Slaughtered cows Shortening, deep frying, soap, candles
Suet Hard white fat (loins/kidneys) Slaughtered cows, calves Pies, mincemeat, puddings
Rennet Enzyme from calf stomach Slaughtered calves Certain cheeses (Parmesan, Gruyère, etc.)
Collagen From skin, bones, tendons Slaughtered cows, calves Supplements, gummies, protein bars
Stearic Acid/Stearin Fatty acid from animal fat Slaughtered cows Candy, gum, chocolate, baked goods
Oleic Acid (animal) Fat from animal tissue (rarely labeled as such) Slaughtered cows, buffalo Some processed foods, cosmetics
Glycerin (animal) Fat breakdown product Slaughtered cows Processed foods, pills, supplements
Mono-/Diglycerides (animal) Emulsifiers from animal fat Slaughtered cows Baked goods, margarines, snacks
Blood Meal Dried blood (rare in human food) Slaughtered cows Fertilizers, animal feed
Bone Char Charred bones used to filter/refine Slaughtered cows Some white cane sugars (not on label)
Bone Meal Ground bone Slaughtered cows, buffalo Supplements, fertilizers, pet food
Meat/Bone Meal Rendered meat & bone byproducts Slaughtered cows, buffalo Processed/pet foods
Pepsin Enzyme from stomach lining Slaughtered calves, cows, pigs Some cheeses, supplements
Lipase (animal) Enzyme from animal organs Slaughtered cows, calves Specialty cheeses, processed foods
Liver Extract Flavoring, supplements Slaughtered cows, calves Supplements, flavorings
Spleen Extract Protein/iron supplement Slaughtered cows Supplements, nutraceuticals
Cartilage Joints/connective tissue Slaughtered cows, calves Supplements, bone broth
Hydrolyzed Animal Protein Broken down muscle or connective tissue Slaughtered cows, buffalo Broths, gravy, soup bases
Animal Digest Enzymatically digested tissue Slaughtered cows, buffalo Pet food, flavoring
Oleo Stearin/Oleo Oil Solid portion of beef fat Slaughtered cows Margarine, shortening, soap
Beef Broth/Stock Boiled meat/bones Slaughtered cows Soups, ready meals, sauces
Extract of Beef/Meat Meat byproduct for flavoring Slaughtered cows, calves Stock cubes, bouillon, noodles

Note: This table excludes dairy-only ingredients (whey, casein, butter, milk fat, lactose, etc.) which don't require slaughter. When in doubt, look for vegan or vegetarian certification, and contact the manufacturer if the origin isn’t clear.
